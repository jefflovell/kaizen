What Is KSP?

A rocket game with suspiciously honest physics.

Kerbal Space Program is a spaceflight sandbox where building the rocket is only half the joke. The other half is learning that orbit is not “going up.” Orbit is falling around a body fast enough that you keep missing the ground. KSP is good at teaching this because it makes you fly the mistake, watch the map, and then fix the mistake with a burn you understand a little better than last time.

Key Terms

Words mission control keeps saying while pretending this is fine.

KSP terminology reference

Apoapsis

The highest point of your orbit. This is where you loiter, plan, and wonder why periapsis looks offended.

Periapsis

The lowest point of your orbit. This is where speed gets spicy and terrain gets legally relevant.

Prograde

Burn with your motion. It stretches the far side of the orbit and usually makes the map look more ambitious.

Retrograde

Burn against your motion. It lowers the far side, slows you down, and is how many safe ideas begin.

Delta-v

Your budget for changing velocity. Fuel is money; delta-v is what the mission actually spends.

Inclination

The tilt of your orbit. Equatorial, polar, retrograde: same planet, different bill from physics.

Transfer orbit

A temporary path between two useful orbits. It is the hallway, not the apartment.

Sphere of influence

The region where one body dominates your motion. Cross the border and a new gravity boss enters the chat.

Single planet systems

Orbit design around one host body and its captured satellites.

Kerbin orbit changes, Mun and Minmus transfers, Duna to Ike, relay shells, polar scans, geostationary mischief, and the small local problems that teach the big interplanetary ones.
