Flight manual // The planet is already moving

Latitude chooses the planes. Longitude chooses the appointment.

A launch site rides east with its rotating body. Near the equator that surface velocity can become almost pure prograde help. Farther north or south, the same eastward motion sits at an angle to many target planes: some helps, some must be cancelled, and some inclinations cannot be reached directly from that latitude at all.

Latitude sets the floor

A direct launch cannot reach a prograde inclination smaller than the site's absolute latitude. You can wait for the right node, but no amount of scheduling persuades geometry to move the launch pad south.

Longitude sets the clock

Longitude does not change the size of the rotational boost. It changes when the desired orbital plane passes over the site—which is Mission Control language for “the launch window was eleven seconds ago.”

Rotation is a vector

Eastward surface speed only helps in proportion to the launch direction. A retrograde target first asks you to cancel the free eastward velocity, then invoice the rocket for the velocity it actually wanted.

Other worlds still spin

Custom bases use the selected body's radius and sidereal day. Eve may offer a little rotational credit while the atmosphere takes your vehicle, confidence, and the last healthy branch of your family tree.

Kerbal field note

This is ideal launch geometry and horizontal rotational credit—not total ascent delta-v. Gravity loss, drag, steering loss, terrain, thrust-to-weight ratio, and whatever structural event just occurred remain your responsibility.
