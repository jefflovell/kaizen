# Kaizen

**Kaizen** (改善) is the Japanese philosophy of continuous improvement:
small, deliberate changes compounding into something durable. Popularized
through the Toyota Way and lean manufacturing, it is less about chasing
perfection than building the systems, habits, and clarity that make progress
repeatable.

Throughout my career, I have had wild successes and gut-wrenching failures. I
have had to pivot, restart, reframe, and relearn. I have been the program
manager and the product manager, the designer and the developer, a founder and
an employee. I am paid to lead others, but I remain—eternally—the student.

My path has led me to scrappy startups, business consulting, the recording
industry, Hollywood, and global enterprise technology. Currently, I am a
technical program leader and organizational architect working at the
intersection of machine learning and artificial intelligence, product,
engineering, and execution at enterprise scale. I build the operating systems
that help complex technical organizations move with clarity, autonomy, and
purpose.

This website is a collection of field notes, interactive explainers, hard-won
lessons, professional philosophy, personal musings, and experiments in making
complex systems easier to understand—and improve.

The site is intentionally lean and dependency-free: vanilla HTML, CSS, and
JavaScript wherever possible, served directly through GitHub Pages. Good music
does not have to be complicated.  I hope you enjoy exploring it as much as I've enjoyed designing and building it.

Cheers,

Jeff
